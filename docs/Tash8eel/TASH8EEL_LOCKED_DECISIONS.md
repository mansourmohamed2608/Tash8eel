## 1. PRODUCT IDENTITY — LOCKED

### 1.1 Product category

Tash8heel AI is an **AI-driven merchant operating system / AI brain for merchant operations**.

It is **not**:

- just a chatbot
- just a WhatsApp bot
- just a POS
- just a CRM
- just a dashboard SaaS
- just a Copilot wrapper

### 1.2 What the AI brain means

The AI brain is the deeper:

- planner
- orchestration layer
- approvals layer
- control layer
- anomaly layer
- forecasting / automation intelligence layer

Copilot is **one interface**, not the whole AI brain.

### 1.3 Core product scope

Tash8heel AI materially spans these domains:

- orders
- cashier / POS
- inventory
- delivery
- finance / FMS
- customer communication
- calls / call center
- campaigns / growth
- automations
- forecasting
- reporting
- branch operations
- HQ / franchise governance
- command center / control plane

### 1.4 Customer-facing channels are core, not side features

Customer communication and order-taking across:

- WhatsApp
- Instagram
- Facebook / Messenger
- phone calls

are a core part of the system and must always be treated as part of the product’s real value.

## 8. UX / PRODUCT DIAGNOSIS — LOCKED

### 8.1 Main diagnosis

The product has a serious identity split:

- technically: it is a merchant operating system
- visually/structurally: it still presents too much like an AI startup demo

### 8.2 Main UX problem

The dashboard and overall structure currently answer the wrong questions.

The system should answer quickly:

1. What is happening right now?
2. What needs my attention?
3. What did the system do for me?
4. Is the business healthy today?

### 8.3 What is wrong with the current product direction

Locked diagnosis from earlier chats:

- dashboard too overloaded
- command center too buried / incorrectly positioned
- sidebar too long and structurally flat
- AI is too visible as vocabulary and too weakly visible as operational consequence
- product feels built to impress a technical evaluator more than serve a daily merchant operator
- onboarding and first-time clarity are weak
- the current product structure behaves too much like a feature list

## 9. HIGH-LEVEL DESIGN TRUTHS — LOCKED

### 9.1 Dashboard principle

The dashboard must function as a **daily briefing**, not a feature showcase or analytics dump.

### 9.2 Navigation principle

Navigation must be:

- grouped
- role-aware
- workflow-first
- operator-readable within seconds

### 9.3 AI surfacing principle

AI should be shown through:

- recommendations
- actions
- surfaced risks
- automation outcomes
- forecast signals
- anomaly flags

and **not** through decorative AI chrome or vague “AI is active” language.

### 9.4 Interface principle

The product must feel:

- calm
- inspectable
- accountable
- Arabic-first
- RTL-native
- operational
- premium through consistency, not decoration

### 9.5 Data presentation principle

The product should prefer:

- tables
- queues
- operational clarity
- freshness indicators
- explicit status semantics

over:

- decorative cards
- flashy charts-first layouts
- feature-list presentation
- decorative gradients

### 9.6 Visual reject truth

The current black “AI demo” visual language should be replaced by a more operational system direction.

## 10. RESTRUCTURE DIRECTION — LOCKED

### 10.1 Restructure is required

The product needs full-system restructuring, not page-level cosmetic improvement.

### 10.2 Restructure goals

The restructure must define:

- product architecture
- information architecture
- module hierarchy
- navigation structure
- dashboard hierarchy
- operator flows
- owner/admin flows
- what to merge
- what to split
- what to hide
- what to delay
- what to rename

### 10.3 Core structural principle

The system must be organized around:

- operational workflows
- role-aware relevance
- daily usage patterns
- serious operator/admin needs

not around:

- feature count
- engineering pride
- AI vocabulary
- internal module boundaries

### 10.4 AI principle

The AI should be **felt, not over-announced**.

Show:

- consequences
- decisions
- surfaced risks
- suggested actions
- automated outcomes

Do not overuse:

- “AI active”
- “brain running”
- token counters
- decorative AI banners
- vague AI notices without operational meaning

### 11.1 Expose first

For the Egypt-first wedge, prioritize exposing:

- dashboard / daily briefing
- operations
- cashier / POS
- conversations

### 11.2 Delay / gate carefully

Do not front-load or overexpose:

Command Center is strategically important, but should be:

- not forced as the daily home for every operator
